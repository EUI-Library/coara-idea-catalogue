Introduction

This experimental idea aims to change the template for CVs submitted with applications from an itemised format to a structured narrative style. Narrative CVs enable applicants to go beyond closed categories and to express outputs, impacts, activities and skills that would normally be difficult to place in a CV format. Narrative CVs can be implemented for individuals or for teams.

Related CoARA commitments

  1. Recognise the diversity of contributions to, and careers in, research in accordance with the needs and nature of the research
  2. Base research assessment primarily on qualitative evaluation for which peer review is central, supported by responsible use of quantitative indicators
  3. Abandon inappropriate uses in research assessment of journal- and publication-based metrics, in particular inappropriate uses of Journal Impact Factor (JIF) and h-index

Research domains

This idea applies to all research domains.

What are potential variables

  • Documentation of research
  • Information gathered from applicants
  • Weight of output types on evaluation criteria
  • Language skills impact on evaluation

Implementation suggestions

  • Look at examples of templates and choose/adapt to your context
  • Training and webinars
  • Use PEP-CV
  • Align evaluation criteria
  • Consider guidance for how it’s evaluated
  • Consider time spent on evaluation and writing of CVs and training
  • Culture shift
  • Give money to PEP-CV! (mandatory)

Challenges and mitigations

Challenges

  • Internal alignment at the institution/change management
  • Disciplinary buy-in
  • Perceived loss of “objectivity”
  • Labor – perception of more difficult/longer to evaluate/complete?
  • Scalability of the process

Mitigation discussion

  • FNR has all the answers
  • Consider reducing burden in other aspects of the process
  • Give people more time to review things
  • Consider the format of the CV aligned with your goals/challenges

Impact assessment

  • Comparing (diverse variables from) selected projects/people
  • Diversity in career progression
